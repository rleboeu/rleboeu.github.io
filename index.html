Professional Life

I'm currently a college student at Midlands Technical College (soon to be USC Columbia) in the Engineering department. Now, I'm terrible when it comes to calculus, which is part of the reason I elected to major in Computer Science. Another aspect of my choice stems from the fact that I've been coding since sophomore year of high school. I started with Java, went to C/C++ during my stint at Clemson, completed a majority of Project Euler with Python, and am back to Java at MTC. I still write SHELL and Python scripts in my free time: How else would I automate everything on my Linux install?

In the future, I hope to graduate with a B.S. in Computer Science from USC. From there, I plan to get an internship with a company (my current two prospectives are Blue Cross Blue Shield and Dominion Energy), and then sign on full-time if I enjoy the work environment but also the work itself. I was fortunate enough to intern with Dominion Energy already; I worked as a Software Engineer Intern for IST Fossil/Hydro at the Cayce, SC location. I loved the people there and the work was immersive even though I had no prior experience with any of the technologies (ASP.NET, C#, SQL Server). I was lucky enough to actually get my own written code pushed into production! It was a great learning experience and I would go back in a heartbeat.
